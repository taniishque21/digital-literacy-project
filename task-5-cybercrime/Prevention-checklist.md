## Stay Safe Online: Checklist for College Students in India

A practical guide to protect yourself from cyber threats, scams, and online fraud.  

---

## 1. General Online Safety

- *Use strong passwords*  
  Create unique passwords using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

- *Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)*  
  Add an extra layer of security to your accounts.

- *Do not share personal information online*  
  Avoid posting sensitive details like address or phone number.

- *Be careful with unknown links*  
  Do not click on suspicious links from emails or messages.

- *Download apps only from trusted sources*  
  Use official app stores like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.

- *Keep your devices updated*  
  Regular updates help fix security issues.

- *Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks*  
  Do not access banking or personal accounts on public networks.

- *Check website authenticity*  
  Ensure websites start with https:// before entering details.

- *Log out from shared devices*  
  Always sign out after using public or shared systems.

- *Be aware of phishing scams*  
  Do not trust messages pretending to be from banks or companies.

---

## 2. Financial / UPI Safety

- *Never share your UPI PIN or OTP*  
  Banks never ask for this information.

- *Do not accept unknown payment requests*  
  Approving a request sends money, not receives it.

- *Be cautious with QR codes*  
  Always verify before scanning any QR code.

- *Verify before sending money*  
  Double-check the receiver’s UPI ID and name.

- *Report fraud immediately*  
  Visit [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) or call *1930*. 

> Report phishing, fraud, or financial scams immediately to protect yourself and others.  

---

## REMINDER

Always stay alert online. Protect your accounts, verify unknown requests, and report suspicious activity immediately. Prevention is the best defense against cybercrime.
